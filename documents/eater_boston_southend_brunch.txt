Source: Eater Boston — "The Best Brunch Spots in the South End and Back Bay"
URL: https://boston.eater.com/

The South End is Boston's brunch heartland, a grid of brownstones packed with cafes and bistros that fill up fast on weekend mornings. Here's where the lines are worth it.

Mike & Patty's (Bay Village). A tiny corner shop legendary for breakfast sandwiches. The "Fancy" — egg, cheese, avocado, bacon — has a cult following. Expect a line out the door and limited seating; most people take it to go. Cheap, fast, and beloved.

The classic South End bistros. Several brunch-focused bistros along Tremont and Washington do elevated egg dishes, benedicts, and pastries. Reservations help on weekends, and walk-ins should brace for a 30-to-45-minute wait at peak. The neighborhood's pretty streets make the wait more pleasant than most.

Cafe culture. Independent cafes throughout the South End serve excellent coffee and pastries for a lower-key morning. Good for a quick, cheaper alternative to a sit-down brunch.

Back Bay options. Around Newbury and Boylston, brunch skews toward hotel restaurants and stylish all-day cafes. Pricier and more polished, convenient if you're shopping or staying nearby.

Vegetarian and vegan brunch. The South End has solid plant-based options too, with several spots offering tofu scrambles and vegan pastries alongside the classics. A good area for mixed groups.

When to go. Weekend brunch peaks between 10 a.m. and 1 p.m. Arrive before 10 or after 1:30 to dodge the worst waits, or go on a weekday for a relaxed table. Many of the best spots don't take reservations, so timing is everything.

Bottom line. For an iconic cheap bite, Mike & Patty's. For a sit-down weekend brunch, the South End bistros along Tremont. For convenience near shopping, Back Bay. Come early, expect a wait, and enjoy one of the prettiest neighborhoods in the city while you do.
