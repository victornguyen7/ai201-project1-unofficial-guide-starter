Source: The Food Lens — "Student-Budget Eats: Allston, Brighton, and Cambridge"
URL: https://thefoodlens.com/boston/


The neighborhoods around Boston's colleges are where you eat best for the least money. Here's a budget tour of Allston, Brighton, and Cambridge.

Ramen in Allston. Allston has the densest cluster of ramen in greater Boston. Yume Wo Katare is the famous, line-out-the-door tonkotsu spot — heavy, garlicky, no reservations, cash only, and a 45-minute-plus wait at peak. Ganko Ittetsu nearby is the lighter, shorter-wait alternative that locals lean on for a weeknight bowl. If ramen is the goal, start in Allston.

Korean fried chicken and bubble tea. Allston and Brighton are loaded with Korean spots, bubble tea shops, and casual Asian eateries that keep portions big and prices student-friendly. A great area to wander and graze.

Cambridge: Central Square. For vegetarians, Veggie Galaxy (a full vegetarian diner with vegan options) and Life Alive (bowls and smoothies) are the two names locals recommend most. Both are affordable and crowd-pleasing even for non-vegetarians.

Cambridge: Harvard Square. Clover does cheap, fast vegetarian fritters and seasonal sandwiches, and the square's cafes are great for a long coffee. Plenty of quick, affordable options between the bookshops.

Porter Square. The Japanese food court in the Porter Exchange is a hidden gem — ramen (Santouka), donburi, and small stalls serving honest, affordable bowls. A good rainy-day stop.

Late-night and cheap. Across these neighborhoods, halal carts, pizza slices, and taquerias stay open late for the student crowd, with full meals often under $12.

Takeaway. For ramen, Allston (Yume for the experience, Ganko for the everyday bowl). For vegetarian, Central Square's Veggie Galaxy and Life Alive. For quick and cheap, Harvard and Porter squares. These neighborhoods are where your dollar stretches furthest in the Boston area.
