Allston is best known for its ramen, but the neighborhood's street food and casual scene is just as worth your time while you wait out the line at Yume Wo Katare.

Why Allston for ramen. As nearly every local guide notes, Allston has the densest cluster of ramen shops in greater Boston. Yume Wo Katare is the destination for the rich, garlicky tonkotsu experience — and the famous line that comes with it, often 45 minutes or more with no reservations and cash only. Ganko Ittetsu Ramen nearby offers a lighter Sapporo-style bowl with a shorter wait, and it's the pick for locals who want ramen on a weeknight without the commitment. If you want ramen in Boston, this is the neighborhood to start in, full stop.

Roxy's Grilled Cheese. The gourmet grilled cheese spot (and its food-truck roots) is an Allston staple — the "Mighty Rib Melt" is a local favorite and a solid cheap meal. There's often an arcade-bar crowd, so it doubles as a late-night option.

Halal carts on Brighton Ave. Several carts serve generous chicken-and-rice platters late into the night for under $12. Popular with the student crowd after the bars close, and a reliable, filling, cheap meal when most kitchens have shut down.

Korean and Asian eats. Allston's stretch of Asian restaurants goes well beyond ramen — Korean fried chicken, bibimbap, bubble tea, and hot pot are all within a few blocks. The neighborhood's student population keeps prices down and portions up.

Pizza and late-night slices. Classic cheap-slice spots stay open late to catch the bar crowd. Not destination dining, but exactly what you want at midnight.

Coffee and study spots. Between the cafes along Harvard Ave, you can nurse a coffee for hours, which is handy if you're killing time before a ramen reservation you couldn't make (because nobody takes them).

The food-truck angle. On warmer days, trucks rotate through the Allston/Brighton area and the nearby campuses, serving everything from tacos to dumplings. Follow the trucks on social media for locations, since they move day to day.

Bottom line. Come to Allston for the ramen — Yume for the iconic, heavy, line-out-the-door experience, Ganko for the lighter everyday bowl — and stick around for cheap grilled cheese, halal platters, and Korean fried chicken. It's the best neighborhood in Boston for eating well on a student budget.
