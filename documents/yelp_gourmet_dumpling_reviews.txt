★★★★★ "The soup dumplings (xiao long bao) are the reason to come. Thin skin, hot broth inside, super cheap for how good they are. There's almost always a wait but it moves and it's worth it. Cash is easiest."

★★★★☆ "Reliable, cheap, and filling. The pan-fried pork buns and scallion pancakes are just as good as the dumplings. Took a star off for the cramped seating and the wait at peak times, but the value is unbeatable in Chinatown."

★★★★★ "Brought a group of six and we stuffed ourselves for like $15 a head. Soup dumplings, dumplings, noodles, greens — everything was solid. This is my go-to cheap group meal downtown."

★★★☆☆ "Food is good and cheap but the wait at lunch on a weekend was rough. Put your name down and walk Chinatown for 20 minutes. Worth it for the dumplings, less so if you're in a hurry."

★★★★★ "Best soup dumplings in Boston's Chinatown, full stop. Don't expect ambiance — expect great cheap food and a line that signals you're in the right place. Get the XLB and the pan-fried buns."

★★★★☆ "Consistent over many visits. Prices stay low, portions stay big. The line is the only downside, but it's a good sign. Bring cash and come hungry."

★★★★★ "If you want filling, cheap, authentic Chinatown food, this is it. The dumplings and the scallion pancakes alone are worth the trip, and you'll walk out having spent very little."
