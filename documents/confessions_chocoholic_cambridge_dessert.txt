Source: Confessions of a Chocoholic — "Sweet Stops and Veggie Eats Around Cambridge"

I spend a lot of time eating my way around Cambridge, and people always ask me where to take a vegetarian friend or where to find a good dessert. Here's my running list.

Veggie Galaxy in Central Square is my number one for a meal. It's a fully vegetarian diner, so everyone at the table can order freely, and almost everything can be made vegan. The milkshakes — including the vegan ones — are worth the trip alone, and the brunch is fantastic if you don't mind a weekend wait. I've taken skeptical meat-eating friends here and they always leave impressed. The bakery case by the door is dangerous in the best way.

Life Alive, also in Central Square, is where I go for something lighter — grain bowls, smoothies, and wraps that are vegetarian and vegan friendly. Quick, healthy, and energizing rather than heavy. It's my go-to weekday lunch when I want to feel virtuous before dessert.

Clover in Harvard Square covers the fast, cheap, vegetarian niche — chickpea fritters, seasonal sandwiches, and those rosemary fries I can't stop ordering. Great between errands.

On the dessert side. Cambridge has quietly become a great dessert town. The cafes around Harvard and Central do excellent pastries, and vegan options have multiplied — you can find oat-milk lattes and vegan cookies almost everywhere now. For chocolate specifically, the small-batch shops and cafe counters around the squares are worth a slow afternoon of grazing.

Ice cream. The Cambridge ice cream scene is legendary, with several beloved local scoop shops that always have a line in summer. Most now carry vegan or dairy-free flavors alongside the classics, so a mixed group can all get a cone.

Coffee and study. If you want to linger, the independent cafes around Harvard Square let you nurse a drink and a pastry for hours. Good for reading, people-watching, or planning your next meal.

My standard answer. Whenever someone asks for vegetarian or vegan food in Cambridge, I send them to Veggie Galaxy and Life Alive in Central Square first, with Clover in Harvard Square as the quick, cheap option. For dessert, wander the squares and follow your nose — the chocolate and ice cream around here rarely disappoint.
