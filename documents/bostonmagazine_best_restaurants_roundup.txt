Source: Boston Magazine — Dining: "A Neighborhood-by-Neighborhood Guide to Eating in Boston"
URL: https://www.bostonmagazine.com/restaurants/

Boston is a city of neighborhoods, and where you eat says as much as what you order. Here's a quick tour of who goes where, and for what.

North End. The Italian core of the city, dense with red-sauce restaurants and the famous cannoli rivalry between Mike's Pastry and Modern Pastry. It's also home to Neptune Oyster, widely cited for the best lobster roll in Boston despite high prices and a no-reservations wait. Expect crowds on weekend nights and a festive, touristy energy. Go on a weekday for a calmer meal.

Seaport. The newest dining district, glossy and pricey, anchored by modern seafood spots like Row 34 and a wave of see-and-be-seen restaurants. Easier reservations than the North End, higher average check.

Chinatown. The city's best value, full stop. Soup dumplings at Gourmet Dumpling House, weekend dim sum at Hei La Moon and China Pearl, cheap bakery snacks on Beach Street, and big bowls of pho — all for very little money. The neighborhood to know when you want to eat well on a budget.

South End. Brunch central, with pretty brownstone streets, beloved breakfast sandwiches at Mike & Patty's, and a strip of bistros that fill up on weekend mornings. Also a strong neighborhood for vegetarians.

Allston and Brighton. Student territory and the city's ramen capital, led by Yume Wo Katare's famous line and the lighter Ganko Ittetsu nearby. Cheap, casual, and open late.

Cambridge. Across the river, Central Square is a haven for vegetarians (Veggie Galaxy, Life Alive), Harvard Square has quick bites like Clover, and Porter Square hides a Japanese food court with excellent ramen. A great-value dining scene with a student discount energy.

The takeaway. For seafood and Italian, the North End. For modern and polished, the Seaport. For cheap and excellent, Chinatown. For brunch, the South End. For ramen and student eats, Allston. For vegetarian, Cambridge. Pick the neighborhood that matches your mood and you'll eat well anywhere in Boston.
