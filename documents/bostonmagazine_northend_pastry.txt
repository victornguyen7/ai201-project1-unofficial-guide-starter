Source: Boston Magazine — Dining: "Mike's vs. Modern: The North End Pastry Debate"
URL: https://www.bostonmagazine.com/restaurants/

No North End rivalry inspires more debate than Mike's Pastry versus Modern Pastry, two cannoli institutions sitting almost across the street from each other on Hanover Street.

Mike's Pastry is the tourist magnet, instantly recognizable by its blue-and-white boxes and the long line snaking out the door on weekends and evenings. The good news: the line is famously fast-moving, and the counter staff are practiced at pushing crowds through quickly. Regulars suggest visiting on a weekday afternoon to skip the worst of the crowd. The ricotta cannoli, filled to order so the shell stays crisp, is the classic pick, and the case is enormous — lobster tails, florentines, and dozens of cookies. Critics argue Mike's has become overpriced and coasts on its reputation, and that the sheer volume means it's more factory than artisan. Defenders counter that the cannoli are still excellent and the experience is part of any first trip to the North End.

Modern Pastry, just steps away, has a devoted following that swears its shells are crispier and its cannoli less sweet. Lines tend to be shorter, though the shop is smaller inside and tends to be cash-friendly. The lobster tail (sfogliatella) is a standout, and many locals quietly consider Modern the connoisseur's choice. The trade-off is a smaller selection and a less flashy room.

When to go. The North End is busiest on Friday and Saturday nights and on summer weekends, when both shops can have lines 20 to 30 minutes deep. Weekday afternoons are the sweet spot. After a big Hanover Street dinner, expect the post-dinner dessert rush around 8 to 10 p.m.

The third option. For zero wait, Bova's Bakery on Salem Street is open 24 hours and has a loyal local base. It rarely has a line, even at 2 a.m., which makes it the insider pick when you don't want to queue. The cannoli are solid and the late-night convenience is unbeatable.

Beyond cannoli. The North End is about more than pastry. For a sit-down dinner, the neighborhood is packed with red-sauce Italian spots, and reservations or early arrivals are essential on weekends. Many visitors pair a pasta dinner with a cannoli walk afterward, joining the Mike's or Modern line for dessert.

The verdict. Among locals, the consensus is that tourists flock to Mike's and the wait can run 20 to 30 minutes at peak times, but it moves quickly and the cannoli deliver. Many Bostonians quietly prefer Modern for a crisper, less sweet shell. And when the lines are out of control, Bova's is the no-wait fallback. You really can't lose — but go on a weekday if you can.
